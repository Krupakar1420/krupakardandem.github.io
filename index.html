⭐ Capstone Project Franklin University · MS Business Analytics · 2025
Hospital Readmission Analysis & Performance Optimization

Business Problem: Some hospitals have higher readmission rates than expected, creating dual challenges in patient care and hospital management. High readmission rates signal poor patient recovery, drive up treatment costs, reduce hospital efficiency, and risk financial penalties under CMS programs.


Key Research Question: What are the key factors influencing hospital readmission rates, and how can hospitals reduce excess readmissions while maintaining quality care and controlling costs?


Using the FY2026 CMS Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program dataset, I analyzed 18,330+ records across 2,995 hospitals in 51 states — covering 6 medical conditions including AMI, Heart Failure, Pneumonia, COPD, Hip/Knee Replacement, and CABG. The analysis involved data cleaning (handling ~30–35% missing values), exploratory analysis, state-level benchmarking, and condition-level performance comparison to surface actionable insights.

Key Findings
📍
State-Level Variation
Larger states (e.g., TX, CA) account for significantly higher discharge volumes; a few states show consistently elevated excess readmission ratios above national benchmarks.
🫀
Condition-Specific Risk
AMI and Heart Failure conditions are associated with relatively higher readmission ratios. A small subset of hospitals show ratios above 1.2–1.3, flagged as outliers requiring targeted intervention.
📊
Performance Benchmarking
Most hospitals perform near the expected benchmark (ratio ≈ 1.0). Evidence-based discharge planning and post-care follow-up are key levers to reduce readmissions in underperforming facilities.
